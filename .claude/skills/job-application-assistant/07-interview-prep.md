# Interview Preparation Guide — Austen Chen

## STAR Format

Structure answers as: **Situation** (context), **Task** (your responsibility), **Action** (what you did), **Result** (outcome).

Keep answers to 1-2 minutes. Be specific. End with what you learned or would do differently.

## Ready-Made STAR Examples

### 1. Gusto Embedded Payroll — Enterprise Partner BD (Technical Pre-Sales + Revenue)
**S:** Gusto was building its Embedded Payroll product and needed to win large enterprise financial institution partners like Chase and US Bank — sophisticated buyers with complex EPD teams who needed to trust both the technical and commercial case before committing.
**T:** As Partner Solutions Architect, I was the pre-sales technical SME responsible for educating partner engineering teams on payroll fundamentals and API utilization, while simultaneously building the business case for their executive leadership.
**A:** I built tailored demo environments, scoped phased build approaches that matched each partner's technical maturity, and projected revenue lift from the ICP base. I also created AI-based internal resources to streamline demo prep and improve API documentation quality, cutting triaging times by 30%.
**R:** Resulted in $XXXk in ARR within 6 months and a pipeline of enterprise-tier embedded payroll partners. Also internally championed partner feature requests that aligned partner timelines with Gusto's roadmap.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a complex deal you drove end-to-end," "How do you work with technical partners at an enterprise level," "Tell me about a time you had to bridge technical and business stakeholders"

### 2. Singular — Building a 1,000+ Platform ETL Ecosystem ($2m MRR)

**A:** I developed new ETL processes with FAANG and 1,000+ ad platforms, built a globally distributed team of 10+ to manage ongoing data partner relationships and ensure GDPR/COPPA compliance, and worked with customers like Target, Walmart, and LinkedIn to reduce eCPAs by 20%+.
**R:** Drove a $2m MRR lift for Singular's core attribution product. Also built proprietary integrations into ad network CDNs (Amazon S3, GCP, Azure) that enabled Creative IQ — resulting in an 18% lift in seat-based sales.
**Use for:** "Tell me about a time you built something from scratch," "Describe a complex technical partnership," "How do you build and manage a distributed team"

## Common Tough Questions

### "Why did you leave Gusto after less than a year?"
> "The Embedded Payroll division went through a restructuring, which changed the scope of the role significantly. I used the transition to wind down Orihon thoughtfully after five years and to be deliberate about where I went next — I wanted to find a role where I could really build something, not just land somewhere quickly."

### "You've worked across a lot of different industries — are you focused?"
> "I'd describe my through-line as: technical, product-driven partnerships at the intersection of business and engineering. Whether it was AdTech at Chartboost, marketing intelligence at Singular, fintech at Gusto, or platform GTM at Autodesk, I've consistently been the person who bridges technical teams and commercial outcomes. Each industry gave me a new context to apply the same core skill set — which means I also bring cross-industry pattern recognition that most specialists don't have."

### "You don't have [specific skill/experience] — how would you handle that?"
> Acknowledge the gap, bridge to adjacent experience, and show the learning curve would be fast: "That's fair — I haven't done X directly, but at [Y company] I did [Z], which is structurally similar. I also learn new technical domains quickly — at Gusto I picked up embedded payroll API patterns within weeks of joining, and at Singular I built out an ETL ecosystem in an entirely new data infrastructure context."

### "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
> "I want to be leading a partnerships or BD function — either as a VP/Head of Partnerships or as part of a founding team building something from the ground up. I'm energized by the 0→1 phase of building: creating the playbook, establishing the ecosystem, and scaling a team around it. The next step is deepening that ownership and doing it at a company with real product-market fit."

### "What's your biggest weakness?"
> "I'm most effective in environments where I have real ownership and can move fast. In highly structured, approval-heavy environments, I sometimes push forward faster than the process allows — which means I've had to learn to bring stakeholders along earlier rather than asking for forgiveness later. I'm much better at this now, but it's something I still consciously manage."

### "Why this company specifically?"
> Customize per application. Must reference a specific: product feature, recent news, market move, or team/person. Never generic. Research the company before every interview.

## Questions You Should Ask Interviewers

### About the Role
- "What does a typical week look like in this role?"
- "What would success look like in the first 6 months?"
- "What's the biggest challenge the team is facing right now?"
- "How much of this role is building new versus managing existing relationships?"

### About the Team
- "How big is the partnerships / BD team, and how is it structured?"
- "Who would I be working most closely with — product, engineering, sales?"
- "How does partnerships interface with the product roadmap?"

### About the Company
- "What's the company's current partnership motion — inbound, outbound, or both?"
- "Where does partnerships sit in terms of strategic priority for the next 12 months?"
- "What does the growth trajectory look like for this team?"

### About Culture
- "What do the people who thrive here have in common?"
- "How would you describe the leadership style in this team?"
- "How much autonomy does this role have over deal structure and strategy?"

## Phone/Video Interview Tips
- Have STAR examples written out (use this file)
- Keep a glass of water nearby
- Smile when speaking (it changes your tone)
- Ask for clarification if a question is vague
- It's OK to take 5 seconds to think before answering
- End with: "Is there anything else you'd like to know about my background?"

## After the Application

### Follow-Up Etiquette
- **Don't call to "stand out"** or learn more about the role post-submission
- If the employer specified a timeline, respect it and wait
- If no timeline was given and 2+ weeks have passed, a brief check-in is acceptable
- If you have genuinely new, relevant information to share, a short follow-up is fine

### Thank-You Notes
- After any interview, send a brief thank-you within 24 hours
- Reference something specific from the conversation
- Keep it short (2-3 sentences) — quality over quantity

## Roleplay Guidelines
When asked for interview practice:
1. Ask which role/company to simulate
2. Start with "Tell me about yourself" — target 90 seconds using the relevant profile statement
3. Progress to role-specific behavioral questions (deal-making, team-building, ambiguity)
4. Include 1-2 technical scoping questions for Solutions Engineer / Partner Engineering roles
5. End with a tough question (gap question, industry-switching question, or weakness)
6. After each answer, give brief feedback: what worked, what to sharpen
7. Suggest which STAR example fits best for each question
