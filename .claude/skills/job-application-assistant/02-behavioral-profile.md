# Behavioral Profile — Austen Chen

## Overview
Austen's behavioral profile is that of a **Builder/Catalyst** — a high-energy, entrepreneurial operator who thrives in ambiguous environments, learns quickly, and brings the drive and effort needed to create something from nothing. Two-time founder with a consistent track record of pioneering first-of-kind partnerships and programs across multiple industries.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Dominance (results/urgency) | High | Action-oriented; moves fast, takes ownership, pushes for outcomes |
| Extraversion (social/influence) | High | Builds relationships naturally; effective at executive interfacing and cross-functional alignment |
| Patience (consistency/pace) | Low-Moderate | Thrives on variety and new challenges; may find repetitive maintenance work draining |
| Formality (structure/process) | Low-Moderate | Creates structure where none exists, but prefers operating with autonomy over rigid process |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **First-mover instinct:** Consistently pioneers new programs, processes, and partnerships before they become standard practice (e.g., first lead gen process at Chartboost, first NFT/blockchain art collective, first ETL integrations with FAANG at Singular)
- **Ambiguity tolerance:** Comfortable in 0→1 situations without a defined playbook; creates clarity through action
- **Rapid learning across domains:** Has successfully operated in fintech, adtech, martech, art, and Web3 — picking up technical and domain context quickly in each
- **Executive presence:** Consistently worked directly with co-founders, C-Suite, and enterprise decision-makers (Chase, US Bank, Autodesk leadership)
- **Cross-functional influence:** Drives alignment across engineering, product, sales, and marketing without direct authority

## How You Work Best
- In roles with real ownership — a clear domain to build and grow, not just execute a playbook
- At the intersection of technical and business — roles that require both understanding how products work and knowing how to sell/partner around them
- In high-growth or early-stage environments where speed matters and impact is visible
- With access to senior leadership — able to move faster and have higher impact with executive buy-in
- Alongside other builders — peers who are driven, curious, and not protective of their turf

## Growth Areas (framed positively for applications)
- **Depth vs. breadth:** Austen's career spans many verticals — when asked, frame this as pattern recognition and cross-industry POV, not lack of focus
- **Structured process-following:** Prefers creating systems over following them — position as a strength in roles that need someone to build out the function

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "build from scratch", "0 to 1", "define the strategy", "first of its kind"
- "entrepreneurial", "founder mindset", "high ownership"
- "fast-paced", "high-growth", "ambiguous environment"
- "cross-functional", "executive-level", "C-Suite"
- "technical BD", "solutions architecture", "partner engineering"
- "launch", "pioneer", "establish"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not a deal-breaker, but worth investigating):
- "maintain and optimize existing partnerships" (heavy maintenance emphasis — would need some build component)
- "follow established playbook" or "manage existing book of business" (without growth component)
- "highly structured / process-heavy organization" (fine if there's autonomy within it)

## Management Style Preferences
- **Works best with:** Leaders who give clear goals and autonomy on the how; who trust their team and act as sponsors vs. micromanagers
- **Gets frustrated by:** Excessive approvals, slow-moving bureaucracy, or leaders who don't advocate for their team cross-functionally

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Lead with the builder/founder angle — the instinct to create something new, not just manage the existing. Use phrases like "defining the playbook," "pioneering," and "from the ground up"
- **CV:** Emphasize first-of-kind achievements in every role; use metrics that show growth created, not just maintained
- **Interviews:** Default to STAR examples that show initiative in ambiguous situations. Lead with Gusto (technical + enterprise BD) and Singular ($2m MRR, distributed team) for senior BD/Partnerships roles
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid claiming deep engineering expertise — the strength is the ability to bridge technical and business, not to build the product itself
